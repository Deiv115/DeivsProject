What is Lean Six Sigma?

Lean Six Sigma is a methodology that creates projects or team efforts to improve performance and decrease defects, costs and waste. It’s the combination of lean manufacturing (quality control by reducing waste and continuous improvement) and Six Sigma (set of techniques and tools for process improvement).

Lean Six Sigma Belts

The Six Sigma Belts follow an order of responsibility to the project and how much knowledge they have:

  • Six Sigma White Belt is the most basic certification. They engage with local teams that assist projects and are not directly involved.
  • Six Sigma Yellow Belt holders are specialists that support project teams on problem-solving tasks in the project.
  • Six Sigma Green Belt holders understand advanced analysis and can resolve problems that affect quality. They lead projects and assist black belts with data collection and analysis.
  • Six Sigma Black Belts are experts and agents of change. They provide training and lead projects.
  • Six Sigma Master Black Belt is the highest level. They will shape strategy, develop key metrics, act as a consultant, and coach black and green belts.

According to Salary.com an average income for a Six Sigma Green Belt holder is $110,800 in the United States.

Lean Six Sigma Professional Certification vs University Certification

In order to receive a professional certification, you need to take an exam from a recognized institution. There are purely professional certifications from independent entities and there are university level certifications; they are both valid options for most of the industries. However, you should always check with your employer and peers what is the most adequate certification for your needs. The most internationally recognized certifications are provided by ASQ and IASSC. Therefore, when studying Lean Six Sigma courses, always focus on picking courses that align with those exams. Also, different certifications will have different requirements, for example: ASQ Green Belt Certification requires 3 years of professional experience in a Six Sigma project while IASSC doesn’t require any previous experience to take the exam.

1. Lean Six Sigma Green Belt Certification (TUM)

My first pick for the best Six Sigma course is for the Lean Six Sigma Green Belt Certification by Technical University of Munich (TUM).

This free-to-audit with limited access specialization from one the leading universities in Europe will provide you with relevant tools for your Six Sigma projects. You will learn the specific tasks, receive a project storybook template to document your results with graded reviews of your documentation after each DMAIC phase, and attend weekly live online sessions for your questions.

Paying learners will receive a TUM Lean Six Sigma Yellow Belt Certification by completing the first three courses. After earning your Yellow Belt Certificate, you can start your Green Belt Certification project in your final project.

No previous experience is required to take this specialization, although some understanding of statistics and quality management will be helpful.

What You’ll Learn

Course 1: Lean Production – An introduction to the Six Sigma methodology and the DMAIC cycle for process improvement with a focus on the Define and Measure phases, including basic statistics for understanding sampling plans and calculating process capability.

Course 2: Six Sigma: Analyze, Improve, Control – Learn how to statistically analyze process data to determine the root cause and propose solutions for process problems, to implement quality management tools, such as 8D and the 5 Whys, and to understand the concept of Design for Six Sigma (DFSS).

Course 3: Six Sigma: Define and Measure – Learn how to apply key elements of Lean Production, from minimizing inventory and reducing setup times, to using 5S and Kaizen, in order to improve quality and productivity in your workplace.

Final Project: Lean Six Sigma: Green Belt Certification Project – Implement a Lean Six Sigma improvement project along with the DMAIC phases, from its identification to the evaluation of its result, to earn the TUM Lean Six Sigma Green Belt certification.

How You’ll Learn

Each course is 6-8 week long and the Green Belt Certification Project is 20 weeks long. Each week is concluded with a graded assignment. Some additional activities are peer-reviewed which require other students’ participation. In order to receive your certificate, you need to pass the exams with a score of 70% or higher.

Institution Technische Universität München (Technical University of Munich)

Level Intermediate
Workload 42 weeks long, 3-4 hours a week

2. Six Sigma Yellow Belt (University System of Georgia)

My second pick for the best Six Sigma course is for the Six Sigma Yellow Belt specialization from the University System of Georgia.

This free-to-audit course is built around the latest handbook The Certified Six Sigma Handbook (2nd edition) and will provide you with the necessary tools to take your professional accreditation. If you are interested in taking the Yellow Belt exam, it’s recommended that you purchase the handbook.

This Specialization does not give the learner “professional accreditation” in Six Sigma. However, successful completion will assist in better preparation for such professional accreditation testing. No previous experience is required to take this specialization.

What You’ll Learn

Course 1: Six Sigma Principles – You will have an introduction to Six Sigma, principles, roles and responsibilities, and understand its purpose and value to an organization. You’ll also learn the basic principles of Six Sigma and Lean. Then, you will have an application using some of the tools and metrics that are critical components of Six Sigma.

Course 2: Six Sigma Tools for Define and Measure – You’ll learn about the first two phases of the DMAIC process (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, and Control). Also, you’ll learn how to develop and implement a Six Sigma project, become familiar with project management tools, and you’ll be introduced to statistics and understand its significance to Six Sigma. Finally, you’ll learn about data collection and its importance.

Course 3: Six Sigma Tools for Analyze – You’ll learn about the third phase of the DMAIC process.  You will learn about lean tools for process analysis, failure mode and effects analysis (FMEA), measurement system analysis (MSA) and gauge repeatability and reproducibility (GR&R), and you will be introduced to basic statistics. You’ll also learn useful measure and analysis phase tools and get an overview of statistics with example problems.

Course 4: Six Sigma Tools for Improve and Control – Learn the tools necessary to complete the final components of the analyze as well as improve and control phases of the DMAIC process. You will learn about relationships from data using correlation and regression as well as the different hypothesis terms in hypothesis testing. This course will provide you with tools and techniques for improvement. You will also understand the importance of a control plan, as well as its key characteristics, for maintaining process improvements.

How You’ll Learn

Each course is divided into 4-5 modules (weeks) which include readings, videos, and a quiz to practice what you’ve learned and text your knowledge. There are also peer review exercises to give you opportunities to apply the materials to your daily life.

If you reach a minimum grade of 50%, you will be automatically awarded an electronic certificate of Accomplishment (White Belt). The course allows you to learn about Lean Sigma and also gives you a glimpse of what ATU’s award-winning online courses are like.

Statistics Foundations 3 – Using Data Sets: Go beyond the basics of statistics with practical, example-based lessons to learn how data sets and statistics are used in the real world.

Statistics Foundations: 3: Complete your mastery of statistics. Get an advanced understanding of concepts such as t-distribution, degrees of freedom, chi-square testing, regression testing, and ANOVA.

Learning Minitab: Discover Minitab for statistical analysis and data-driven decision-making. Learn about the charts, graphs, descriptive and inferential statistics features, and reports in Minitab.

Six Sigma: Green Belt: Learn what you need to operate as a Six Sigma Green Belt. This course covers measurement system analysis, descriptive statistics, hypothesis testing, experiment design, and more.

Root Cause Analysis: Getting to the Root of Business Problems: Learn how to solve your business’s problems at their root with sustainable solutions.

How to Manage Lean Six Sigma Projects: Part I: Learn how to identify, select, and plan Lean Six Sigma projects.

How to Manage Lean Six Sigma Projects: Part I: Learn how to manage and run Lean Six Sigma projects in the Analyze, Improve and Control phases of DMAIC in this second part of two courses.

Change Management Foundations: Learn how to assess your organization’s readiness for change and apply a powerful, step-by-step approach to help employees make changes that stick.
