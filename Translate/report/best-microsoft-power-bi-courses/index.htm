What is Microsoft Power BI?

Power Bi is a business intelligence product suite developed by Microsoft. It is used to visualize data by connecting to various data sources and creating rich, interactive business reports that deliver meaningful insights within your data, thereby helping you make better business decisions.

But what exactly is business intelligence? Microsoft states that business intelligence (BI) helps organizations analyze historical and current data, so they can quickly uncover actionable insights for making strategic decisions.

Before using Power BI, it’s good to know what data modeling is. According to Microsoft, data modeling is the process of analyzing and defining all the different data your business collects and produces, as well as the relationships between those bits of data.

Why are Power BI skills important?

Microsoft is named a Leader in the 2022 Gartner® Magic Quadrant™ for Analytics and Business Intelligence Platforms.

Forrester recognizes Microsoft as a Leader in The Forrester Wave™: Augmented BI Platforms, Q3 2021. According to Forrester, in 2020, 49% of data and analytics decision-makers at firms with an advanced level of insights-driven business maturity expect their organization to use augmented BI platforms, compared with only 31% at firms with a beginner level of maturity.

In 2020, two-thirds of data and analytics decision-makers reported that their organization was recruiting more talent with advanced data and analytics skills. Moreover, data and analytics decision-makers said their organization allocated as much as 45% for combined investments in reporting, dashboards, performance management, data exploration, and OLAP.

“It is hard not to consider Power BI as your top choice for an enterprise BI platform, especially if your organization plans to deploy most of its business and productivity applications and store and process most of the data on Microsoft Azure”.

2. Microsoft Power BI Desktop for Business Intelligence (Udemy)

My second pick for the best Microsoft Power BI online course is Microsoft Power BI Desktop for Business Intelligence on Udemy.

Designed for Windows users, this course is a comprehensive, hands-on guide to learning Microsoft Power BI Desktop. This is a project-based course, and the instructors will guide you through each step using detailed explanations and helpful pro tips.

What You’ll Learn

First, you’ll learn how to connect and transform the raw data. You’ll be introduced to the Power BI Query Editor, types of Power BI data connectors, basic table transformations, text, number and date tools, and index and conditional columns.

You’ll also learn about grouping and aggregating data, pivoting and unpivoting, modifying, merging and appending queries, connecting to folders, defining hierarchies and categories, query editing, and Power BI best practices.

Next, you’ll build a relational data model. You’ll be introduced to database normalization, data (“fact”) tables vs. lookup (“dimension”) tables, create Power BI table relationships, and learn about  “star” vs. “snowflake” schemas.

Also, you’ll learn about active vs. inactive relationships, relationship cardinality, connecting multiple data tables, filtering and cross-filtering, hiding fields from the Power BI report view, and data modeling and Power BI best practices.

After that, you’ll learn to add calculated fields with DAX. You’ll be introduced to Data Analysis Expressions (DAX), calculated columns vs.measures, row context vs.filter context in Power BI, DAX syntax and operators, and common Power BI functions.

You’ll also learn about basic date and time formulas, logical and conditional statements, text, math and stats functions, joining data with RELATED, and work with CALCULATE, ALL and FILTER Functions.

Then you’ll work with DAX iterators (SUMX, AVERAGEX), time intelligence formulas, and learn some of the DAX and Power BI best practices.

Moving on, you’ll learn how to design interactive Power BI reports. You’ll be introduced to the Power BI report view, and learn about adding basic charts to Power BI reports, formatting and filtering options, matrix visuals, slicers and timelines, and cards and KPIs.

You’ll take a look at Power BI map visuals (basic, fill, ArcGIS), treemaps, lines, areas and gauges, and learn about editing report interactions, adding drillthrough filters, linking to report bookmarks, using “What-If” parameters, and managing and viewing roles.

Finally, you learn about publishing to Power BI service and Power BI data viz best practices.

How You’ll Learn
