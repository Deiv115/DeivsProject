What is Revit?

Autodesk Revit is a BIM (building information modeling) software used by architecture, engineering and construction (AEC) teams to create high-quality buildings and infrastructure. You can use Revit to model structures and systems in 3D, streamline documentation, and work with multi-disciplinary teams in a unified project environment.

Revit contains industry-specific tools for architectural design, structural engineering, MEP engineering, and construction. It is used by architects, structural engineers, MEP engineers, builders, fabricators, computational designers, owners and others to design, document, visualize and deliver architecture, engineering and construction projects.

You can get started with Autodesk’s own learning resources here.

Why are Revit Skills Important?

According to a report from Fortune Business Insights, the Building Information Modeling (BIM) market is projected to grow from USD 5.71 billion in 2020 to USD 11.96 billion in 2027 at a CAGR (compound annual growth rate) of 11.1% in the 2020-2027 period. The driving force behind this exponential growth is the rapid urbanization with the growing technological development and improvement of cities.

At the moment, there are over 200 freelance jobs available on Upwork that require Revit knowledge and over 20,900 jobs available on LinkedIn that require Revit knowledge for the USA alone. Personally also, as a civil engineer, I’ve seen jobs that require Revit skills.

Course Ranking Methodology

I built this ranking following the now tried-and-tested methodology I used in previous rankings (you can find them all here). It involves a three-step process:

  1. Research: I started by leveraging Class Central’s database with 100K online courses and 200K+ reviews. Then, I made a preliminary selection of 130+ Revit courses by rating, reviews, and bookmarks.
  2. Evaluate: I read through reviews on Class Central, Reddit, and course providers to understand what other learners thought about each course and combined it with my own experience as a learner.
  3. Select: Well-made courses were picked if they presented valuable and engaging content and they have to fit in a set of criteria and be ranked accordingly: comprehensive curriculum, release date, affordability, ratings and enrollments.

The end result is a unique selection of courses that combines a decade of Class Central data and my own experience as an online learner to try to get the best of both worlds. So far, I’ve spent more than 12 hours building this list, and I’ll continue to update it.

4. BIM Fundamentals for Engineers (National Taiwan University)

My fourth pick for the best Revit online course is the free-to-audit BIM Fundamentals for Engineers, offered by National Taiwan University on Coursera.

This is the only university course to learn Autodesk Revit on this list. You’ll learn the essential concepts of BIM (or building information modeling) and use Revit to create and manipulate a BIM model and retrieve information from it. As a former student of this course, I can say that it will be fairly easy to complete this course if you follow the lectures step by step.

What You’ll Learn

First, you’ll install Revit step by step. Then, you’ll understand the fundamentals of BIM, definition of BIM, transfering from CAD to BIM, necessity of BIM, BIM benefits, and LOD in BIM.

Next, you’ll view and retrieve information from BIM models. You’ll understand view controls such as pan, zoom, rotate and arrange views, visibility of elements, section a 3D view, retrieve information from schedules, and measure distance in BIM models.

Finally, you’ll model a BIM model. You’ll use the Grid tool to place grid lines, Level tool to define a vertical height, Toposurface tool to create topographic surface, Column tool to place a column, Beam tool to place a beam, Floor tool to create a floor, Wall tool to create a wall, Door tool to place a door, Window tool to place a window, Stair tool to create a stair, and Ramp tool to create a parking ramp.

How You’ll Learn

The course is broken down into 5 weeks. Each week involves about 1 to 5 hours of work. Concepts are taught through a combination of videos, readings and quizzes. Peer-graded assignments are also included.
